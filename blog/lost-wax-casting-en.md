## What is Lost Wax Casting?

Lost wax casting is an ancient metal casting technique that originated in Mesopotamia over 5,000 years ago and reached its peak during China's Shang and Zhou dynasties. This process allows for the creation of incredibly detailed and complex metal artworks, and remains the preferred method for high-end copper crafts today.

---

## The Lost Wax Casting Process

### Step 1: Wax Model Creation

Craftsmen sculpt the product prototype using beeswax based on design drawings. This step determines all details of the final product.

### Step 2: Mold Making

The wax model is dipped into refractory slurry multiple times to form a hard outer shell.

### Step 3: Wax Removal

The mold is heated to melt and drain the wax, leaving a precise cavity.

### Step 4: Pouring

Molten copper (approximately 1,100°C) is poured into the mold cavity.

### Step 5: Finishing

After cooling, the mold is broken to remove the copper piece, which then undergoes polishing, buffing, coloring, and other finishing processes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What's the difference between lost wax casting and sand casting?**

A: Lost wax casting offers higher precision, richer details, and is suitable for complex shapes. Sand casting is more cost-effective but better suited for simpler forms.

**Q: How long can lost wax cast copper items last?**

A: With proper care, lost wax cast copper items can last for centuries. Many Shang and Zhou dynasty bronzes remain in excellent condition today.
